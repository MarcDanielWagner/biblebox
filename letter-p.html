Letter Lyrics

Each poem tells a narrative with only one letter of the alphabet;
using only words that begin with that one letter;
never repeating a word twice.

Letter P

Pictures and pearls, in the proceeding pulpit of paragraphs,
prod people to perceive, the polluted prisons of their perverted passions,
producing personal and public poverty,
planting persons on painful paths, pierced and punished,
plunging players into pointless pomp and poison.

No personage profits from the precepts and perfume of pride,
but prostitutes any principles and probity,
parking pandemonium in the privileged and poor,
provoking the Prince of Peace to the precipice,
placing them in padlocks, proper for participation with their parents.

That particular pair of the past, pursuing partnership with the pernicious prophet,
the pretender, who planned to purge from paradise those previous partners,
pushed our predecessors from a position of provision and protection,
propelling them from the Presence, into persistent, precarious places,
plunging and prescribing patrons into profane, putrid practices.

Pilate was poised to pacify the perilous and pathetic pack,
persuaded to put the pristine Potter of piety, permanently in the pit to perish.
Poked and plucked by the pestilence of the partially pious pastors,
the predestined, purposed prophecy, prospered over the preferred protest and panic,
permitting the presumptuous and plenty the present party.

The promised propitiation of this perfect providence,
whose performance prevailed over persecution and powers,
proliferated polished purity, promoting a prepared populace,
primed to partake from the pasture of our Protector and preserver,
the pivotal pillar and pinnacle of positive pleasure.

It is my pride and plentiful, patient prayer,
to propagate and proclaim with prominence,
the precious, pricey, perduring prize
of the preeminent plenary priest,
whose pattern and power, Paul precisely preached.

My paramount petition, is to pour on pressure, in pursuance of the peerless prime Pioneer,
perpetuating and pointing to the praise of his pardon and portion.
Please part from the process and punches of peccancy,
the perplexing plague, that pricks and piles pangs.
This is my perceptive plea, with the letter “P”.
